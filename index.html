Commercial Air Travel: Past to Now

Commercial air travel is a staple part of modern society. It is used for personal trips, like vacations, and business trips by millions of people. The origins of this mode of transportation are an interesting part of history that showcase the progress of the human desire to fly.

The Early Years

Modern flying is traced back to the first flight of the Wright Brothers, Wilbur and Orville. Their successful flight in Kitty Hawk, NC paved the way for aviation as a possible means of travel and provided it as a resrouce for other funtions, such as moving cargo and aerial warfare.

Progress and Development

General commercial avaiation started on January 1st, 1914 with a flight between St. Petersburg and Tampa, FL. This started a series of events that connected the world more seamlessly. A timelines of commercial aviation can be found here.

Modern Flying

Modern flight became a revlutionary method of travel after the first flight of the Concorde.This plane was, and continues to be, the fastest commerical plane in the world with a top speed of Mach 2. The most recognizable indicator of modern flight, however, is the Boeing 747, also known as the Queen of the Skies.
